Overview

ERP.net Apps allow developers to extend, integrate, and automate ERP.net systems using the ERP.net APIs.

They make it possible to build new user experiences, connect ERP.net with other software, and streamline business processes.

Every app - whether internal, external, or marketplace - connects to ERP.net securely through its built-in ERP.net Identity and is represented in the system by a Trusted Application entity.

Together, these components ensure that every app is properly identified, authenticated, and authorized with appropriate access.

What Are ERP.net Apps

An ERP.net App is any application, service, or integration that communicates with an ERP.net instance through its public APIs.

Apps can range from small automation scripts to complete web or mobile solutions built around ERP.net data and business logic.

Examples of ERP.net Apps include:

  • A web store that allows customers to browse products and place orders online
  • A background integration that synchronizes stock levels with an online store
  • A web portal that allows suppliers to update product information
  • An analytics service that extracts and processes ERP data for reporting

Apps can be developed by in-house teams, partners, or independent developers and can operate in private environments or be distributed through the ERP.net Marketplace.

Building Apps for ERP.net

The Building Apps section is your complete guide to developing secure, modern, and scalable applications for ERP.net.

ERP.net provides a unified platform where apps can connect, authenticate, and extend the system safely - whether they are internal extensions, web apps, background services, or external integrations.

What You Can Build

You can build:

  • Interactive web or desktop apps – for users who sign in and work with ERP.net data.
  • Service or background integrations – automated jobs that sync or process data.
  • Hybrid apps – combined solutions with both user-facing and background components.
  • BI dashboards and connectors – systems that analyze ERP.net data through APIs.

Every app connects securely through the ERP.net Identity and uses tokens to access the Domain API or Table API.

Why Build Apps

Apps extend the value of ERP.net beyond its core modules.

They allow organizations to:

  • Tailor ERP.net to their unique business workflows
  • Integrate with external platforms and internal systems
  • Automate routine operations and data flows
  • Deliver focused experiences for specific users or roles

By connecting securely to an ERP.net instance, apps help organizations innovate without altering the base system.

How Apps Work with an ERP.net Instance

Each app communicates with an ERP.net instance through standard APIs that are part of the instance itself.

Before it can do so, the app must be recognized and granted the right level of access.

This is handled by two core components of the platform:

  • The ERP.net Identity, which authenticates users and services and enforces authorization decisions based on instance policies
  • The Trusted Application, which represents the app's registration and configuration inside the instance

When an app needs to access data, it initiates authentication with the ERP.net Identity (either interactively through a user or non-interactively as a service).

After successful authentication and authorization, the app calls the APIs within the instance to read or update ERP.net data in line with its allowed permissions.

Apps as Part of the ERP.net Platform

ERP.net treats every app as a managed, auditable part of the system.

Administrators can see which apps are trusted, what they are authorized to access, and how they interact with the instance.

They can also revoke or adjust access at any time, maintaining full control over integrations.

This model allows ERP.net Apps to be open, extensible, and secure - forming a flexible ecosystem where developers can build new solutions without compromising platform integrity.

Before You Start

  1. Make sure your ERP.net instance is accessible.
  2. Register your app as a Trusted Application in that instance.
  3. Choose the proper authentication flow for your app type.
  4. Start testing API calls using tools like Postman or curl.
